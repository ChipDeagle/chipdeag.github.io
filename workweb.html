Cognitive Musicology

VIEW/HIDE

This website was created to store, organize, and present the course content for the Cognitive Musicology course at SUNY Oswego. This includes small melodies made with the MxM software and the documentation for each piece of work. Additionally, the website also contains a video presentation relevant to Cognitive Musicology and a powerpoint presentation about a leading figure in this field. Some additional resources pertaining to the subject are also listed at the bottom of the page.

Generative Processes and Formal Systems

VIEW/HIDE

This website was created to store, organize, and present the course content for the Generative Processes and Formal Systems course at SUNY Oswego. The work represented on the website includes reading assignments from the book “Gödel, Escher, Bach: an Eternal Golden Braid”, melodies made using Markov Processes and Context Free Grammars (CFGs), a video presentation on Henry Nelson Goodman, a short video on Fuzzy Logic and papers on John Searle’s Chinese Room Argument and human logical thinking.

Computational Models of Cognitive Systems

VIEW/HIDE

This website was created to store, organize, and present the course content for the Computational Models of Cognitive Systems course at SUNY Oswego. Much of the work displayed on this site consists of the use of Prolog in heuristic problem solving. The website also includes reading assignments based on Douglas Hofstadter’s book Fluid Concepts and Creative Analogies and a video presentation about the use of neural networks in gaming. Additional links pertaining to the subject at hand are presented at the bottom of the page for further reading.

Cognitive Science Capstone

VIEW/HIDE

This website was created to store, organize, and present the course content for the Cognitive Science Capstone Seminar course at SUNY Oswego. The work on this website mainly focuses on the process of putting together an empirical literature review of the topic ‘Bilingualism and Cognitive Development’. Additional work includes a number of blog posts, reading assignments based on The Craft of Research by Wayne C. Booth, and reviews of a number of seminal papers on Cognitive Science.
