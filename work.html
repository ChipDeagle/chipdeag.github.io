Bilingualism and Cognitive Development

This literature review investigates if there are any cognitive differences between monolinguals and bilinguals (or multilinguals). It is hypothesized that multilingual individuals will demonstrate an improvement in the development of certain cognitive domains. While past research indicates that multilingualism may be detrimental to cognitive development, later research indicate that multilinguals may be better at mental flexibility and concept formation in childhood than their monolingual peers. Additionally, older multilinguals may be less prone to neurodegeneration than monolingual adults.

Teaching Chess to Neural Nets

This literature review discusses the use of Artificial Neural Network (ANN) technology in the creation of computer chess programs. The paper provides a brief history of computer chess programs, provides a timeline of some of the chess programs that use ANNs and attempt to identify similarities and differences between the techniques used. This information is used to illustrate the extent to which ANNs have developed, and the role Chess has had to play in it.
